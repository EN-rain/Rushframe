# Rushframe Showcase Edit Brief

## Concept

A vertical "camera squeeze" short where the opening faux-camera framing quickly locks onto the face, then sharpens into a graphic portrait payoff with restrained motion and deliberate typography.

## Mood

- curious
- playful
- punchy

## Story progression

- Opening: Immediate close-up hook with faux-camera energy and a short text hit that forces attention to the face.
- Build: Crop, timing, and motion become more controlled while layered text or shape accents make the portrait feel designed rather than raw.
- Payoff: The strongest centered composition lands with the cleanest framing, biggest text emphasis, and the strongest beat accent.
- Ending: A held final portrait frame with a clean fade instead of an accidental cut.

## Visual language

Centered portrait framing, minimal but intentional layering, one consistent type system, and keyframed scale/position motion used only where it supports emphasis.

## Audio language

One main beat-led music bed with restrained accents on structural cuts. Audio should support pacing and end with a deliberate fade.

## Typography direction

Compact bold sans-serif, uppercase for hooks, high contrast, and safe-area-aware placement.

## Color direction

Cool violet-blue base with measured contrast lift and no heavy-handed grading.

## Transition and motion rules

Hard cuts by default. At most one or two purposeful transitions. Motion should be eased and readable, not constant-speed drift.

## Restraints

- Do not use effects only to demonstrate features.
- Do not use identical clip timing throughout.
- Do not place transitions between every cut.
- Do not leave default text, position, or styling.
- Do not exceed 30.00 seconds.

## Intended platform

Short-form vertical social video.

## Output

- Resolution: 1080 x 1920
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Target duration: 20-30 seconds
- Maximum duration: 30.00 seconds
