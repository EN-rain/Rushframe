## Filled Editing Brief

- **Purpose:** Produce a compelling Batman duel highlight that can retain viewers and satisfy the campaign's TikTok clipping requirements.
- **Target audience:** TikTok viewers interested in Batman, action scenes, superhero confrontations, cinematic edits, and dramatic character moments.
- **Platform:** TikTok only.
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical.
- **Preferred resolution:** 1080 × 1920.
- **Minimum duration:** 7 seconds.
- **Target duration:** 12–25 seconds unless the selected source moment needs slightly more time for setup and payoff.
- **Maximum duration:** Keep concise; avoid exceeding 35 seconds without a strong narrative reason.
- **Tone:** Intense, cinematic, confrontational, suspenseful.
- **Editing style:** Social-media highlight with restrained cinematic treatment.
- **Pacing:** Immediate hook, fast setup, escalating duel action, clear payoff, short exit.
- **Hook deadline:** First strong visual, line, impact, threat, reveal, or question within 1.0 second.
- **Required source:** Batman footage from the registered campaign content folder only.
- **Required message:** The clip must clearly communicate a duel, confrontation, threat, reversal, tactical advantage, or decisive Batman moment.
- **Required original contribution:** Add custom high-quality text or captions. Do not merely burn in existing subtitles without editorial transformation.
- **Media to avoid:** External downloads, unrelated footage, unregistered media, duplicated low-quality clips, watermarked reposts, offline assets, and clips outside the campaign content folder.
- **Caption policy:** Use concise, high-contrast, readable captions or editorial text. Keep text inside TikTok-safe areas and avoid covering faces, weapons, or the central action.
- **Music policy:** Use registered music only when it strengthens tension and does not mask dialogue. Source dialogue and action audio remain primary.
- **Sound-effect policy:** Use registered SFX sparingly for one or two motivated impacts, transitions, or reveals. Do not add an effect to every cut.

- **Branding policy:** No invented campaign logo, sponsor claim, payment claim, or official Batman affiliation.

## Required Workflow

### 1. Inspect before editing

Before proposing operations:

1. Read the live project revision.
2. Read the campaign description, structured editing brief, and incomplete tasks.
3. Confirm which registered assets belong to the Batman campaign content folder.
4. Inspect the timeline, tracks, locks, existing edits, transitions, captions, and audio.
5. Inspect transcripts, scenes, moments, duplicate takes, relationships, visual descriptions, dialogue, action tags, camera movement, audio events, and warnings.
6. Search for moments involving Batman and a direct opponent, threat, chase, strike, block, counter, reveal, tactical move, or decisive reaction.
7. Exclude unregistered, offline, unrelated, watermarked, duplicated, or technically poor footage.
8. Confirm that the intended final duration is at least 7 seconds.

Do not select clips based only on filenames.

### 2. Choose one coherent duel story

Build one clear micro-story rather than a random montage:

- **Hook:** Start with the strongest threat, attack, reveal, striking visual, or concise original text.
- **Setup:** Establish who is confronting Batman and what is at stake.
- **Escalation:** Show the strongest connected exchange, tactical move, or reaction.
- **Payoff:** End on the most satisfying hit, counter, escape, reveal, line, or visual reversal.
- **Exit:** Use a brief final hold, reaction, or engagement question when it improves completion rate.

Do not combine unrelated Batman scenes solely because each has a high score.

### 3. Clip-selection rules

Prefer moments that jointly provide:

- Batman clearly visible or unmistakably central
- Immediate conflict or tension
- Complete dialogue clauses
- Strong action/reaction continuity
- Clear visual direction
- Usable source audio
- Shot diversity
- A readable beginning and payoff
- Minimal context required from outside the clip

Avoid:

- Repeated dialogue or duplicate takes
- Cuts inside words or essential reactions
- Unclear geography
- Random opponent changes
- Reversed screen direction without a motivated bridge
- Excessive darkness that makes the action unreadable
- Long setup before conflict
- Dead air
- Near-identical adjacent shots
- Clips under 7 seconds

### 4. Original text and caption requirements

The edit must add original editorial value through text or captions.

Use one or both:

1. **Opening hook text** — short, specific, and tied to the actual scene.
2. **Clean dialogue captions** — corrected, timed, and formatted for readability.

Potential hook patterns, only when supported by the source:

- `BATMAN KNEW THE ATTACK WAS COMING`
- `HE PICKED THE WRONG FIGHT`
- `BATMAN TURNED THE DUEL AROUND IN SECONDS`
- `THE MOMENT BATMAN STOPPED HOLDING BACK`
- `WHO ACTUALLY WON THIS DUEL?`

Do not use a hook that misrepresents the scene.

Caption rules:

- Maximum two lines.
- Prefer 3–7 words per caption event.
- Keep lines concise and balanced.
- Maintain high contrast with outline or shadow.
- Do not obscure faces or major action.
- Keep essential text away from TikTok interface zones.
- Correct obvious transcript errors without changing meaning.
- Avoid excessive emojis, all-caps dialogue, or decorative effects that reduce readability.

### 5. Visual editing rules

- Reframe for 9:16 while keeping Batman, the opponent, and action readable.
- Use position and scale keyframes only when needed to follow the subject.
- Avoid extreme crops that remove the opponent, weapon, movement direction, or payoff.
- Prefer motivated punch-ins rather than constant zooming.
- Preserve screen direction where possible.
- Use cross-asset relationship evidence for matching action, reaction, motion, location, and subject continuity.
- Do not apply random shake, flash, blur, chromatic effects, or speed ramps.
- A speed ramp is acceptable only when it clearly emphasizes a supported impact and keeps the action understandable.

### 6. Audio rules

- Preserve important dialogue, impacts, movement, and reactions.
- Remove only silence that does not contribute to tension.
- Preserve short anticipation pauses and reaction tails.
- Prevent abrupt audio cuts with short fades where necessary.
- Keep added music below dialogue and meaningful source effects.
- Use SFX only when licensed, registered, and motivated.
- Avoid misleading replacement sounds that materially change what occurs in the scene.

### 7. Narrative beat sheet

Create a beat sheet before operations. Preferred structure:

| Beat | Approximate range | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Hook | 0.0–1.0s | Strong visual, threat, impact, or original hook text |
| Setup | 1.0–4.0s | Establish Batman, opponent, and immediate conflict |
| Escalation | 4.0–10.0s | Connected duel exchange, tactical move, or reaction |
| Payoff | 10.0–18.0s | Decisive counter, reveal, strongest line, or impact |
| Exit | Final 0.5–2.0s | Reaction, final hold, or short engagement question |

Adapt the timing to the actual selected footage while keeping total duration at least 7 seconds.

### 8. Atomic plan requirements

Return one coordinated edit plan whenever practical.

The plan must include:

- Current base revision
- Campaign objective
- Assumptions
- Selected Batman media and moment IDs
- Narrative beats
- Ordered operations
- Expected duration
- Text/caption strategy
- Reframing strategy
- Audio strategy
- Affected ranges
- Warnings
- Validation results

Do not use placeholder IDs or unsupported actions.

### 9. Rough-cut review

Before final application, use Rushframe's isolated review workflow when available:

1. Preview the plan.
2. Run `rushframe.review_edit_plan` with `render_draft: true`.
3. Inspect timeline-quality warnings and render verification.
4. Check hook timing, crop, caption readability, repeated material, gaps, transitions, and duration.
5. Submit a small corrected plan against the unchanged live revision.
6. Apply only after user approval.

Do not claim that the clip is high quality until the rough-cut render has been reviewed.
